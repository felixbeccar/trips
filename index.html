Why Asturias in October

Northern Spain at its finest. The summer crowds are gone, the light turns golden, and the mountains do something exceptional in October — chestnuts, beech forests turning amber, and the air that only exists 30km from the Atlantic coast.

You fly from Madrid. Felix lands at Barajas on Aeromexico direct from Monterrey. You pick him up and drive north together — that 5-hour drive through Castilla and over the Puerto de Pajares is part of the experience.

🐴
Horses in the Picos
A proper 3-hour mountain ride with Aravalle Centro Ecuestre — real terrain, real horses, not a tourist walk.
🏔️
Cares Gorge
One of Europe's most dramatic natural landscapes. You walk the first stretch of the gorge, not the full 23km.
🍽️
Asturian table
Fabada, cachopo, Cabrales straight from a cave, cider poured from above your head. One Michelin option if you want it.

Cantabrian coast
Comillas, Santillana del Mar, and Sardinero beach — medieval villages and dramatic Atlantic coastline.
The itinerary

Day by day

Day 01
Arrival

Madrid → Oviedo

~5h drive north · ~690km

Felix lands at Barajas. You pick him up. The drive up through Castilla and over the Cantabrian mountains is the official start of the trip. Arrive in Oviedo mid-afternoon, drop bags, walk the medieval old town.

  • Evening: Calle Gascona — the cider street. Multiple sidrerías, cider poured the traditional way from above the head.
  • Dinner: La Corte de Pelayo for fabada and cachopo, or Casa Fermín (Michelin star, tasting menu ~€80) if you want to start strong.
  • Sleep: Oviedo city center — Hotel de la Reconquista or similar.
Day 02
The main event

Picos de Europa

Oviedo → Arenas de Cabrales · ~1.5h drive

The heart of the trip. Mountains, horses, gorge, cheese. A full day in the Picos with two distinct experiences — morning on horseback, afternoon at the edge of the gorge.

  • 10am — 1pm: 3-hour mountain ride with Aravalle Centro Ecuestre, near Potes. Experienced rider route — real mountain terrain, chestnut forests, panoramic Picos views. Includes picnic lunch.
  • Afternoon: Drive to Poncebos and walk the first 20-30 minutes into the Cares Gorge. The walls are 2,000m high. You get the full picture without doing 23km.
  • Late afternoon: Stop at the Cueva del Quesu in Arenas de Cabrales — buy real Cabrales direct from the cave where it ages.
  • Sleep: Stay near Arenas de Cabrales or Cangas de Onís. Hotel Mirador de la Cepada (4★) if you want comfort.
Day 03
Coast & Cantabria

Green Coast → Santander

Picos → Comillas → Santander · ~2h drive

Leave the mountains and head east along the coast. The Cantabrian Green Coast in October is dramatic — no crowds, cliffs, Atlantic light. Two village stops before landing in Santander for dinner.

  • Morning: Comillas — a small Cantabrian town with Gaudí's El Capricho, a modernist villa most tourists never find. 45 minutes well spent.
  • Midday: Santillana del Mar — one of the best-preserved medieval villages in Spain. No cars, cobblestones, wine in the square.
  • Dinner in Santander: Fresh fish and seafood. El Serbal (Michelin star) if you want a proper evening, or find a good marisquería on the port.
  • Sleep: Santander waterfront — Hotel Bahía or similar.
Day 04
Final morning

Santander → Madrid

~4.5h drive south · ~590km

Slow morning before the road back. The Sardinero is one of the great underrated Spanish beaches — wide, dramatic, completely empty in October. Then the drive home.

  • Morning: Walk Playa del Sardinero. Coffee and sobaos pasiegos — Cantabrian butter pastries. Buy a box for the road.
  • Drive: Back to Madrid on the A-67/A-1. ~4.5 hours. Felix flies home from Barajas. You're in Somosaguas for dinner.
What you'll eat

The Asturian table

🥘
Fabada Asturiana

The regional masterpiece. White fabes beans slow-cooked with chorizo, morcilla, and lacón. Heavy, smoky, deeply satisfying. Have it for lunch, not dinner.

🥩
Cachopo

Two veal escalopes stuffed with ham and local cheese, breaded and fried. One kilo of food. Order one between two people. The 1947 recipe at La Corte de Pelayo is legendary.

🧀
Cabrales

The most powerful blue cheese in Spain, aged in limestone mountain caves. You'll buy it direct from the source in Arenas de Cabrales. Nothing like what you get elsewhere.

🍎
Sidra Natural

Asturian cider, poured from height — the escanciado. Dry, tannic, barely carbonated. The ritual of ordering and pouring is the whole point. Get on Calle Gascona.

🍤
Marisco del Cantábrico

Atlantic seafood in October is superb — percebes, nécoras, merluza. Santander for dinner on Day 3 is the moment for this. Order whatever the waiter recommends.

🥐
Sobaos Pasiegos

Cantabrian butter pastry from the Pas Valley. The best breakfast item in northern Spain. Buy a box in Santander before the drive back on Day 4.

Where to eat

Recommended tables

Night 1 · Oviedo
Casa Fermín
Michelin Star · Tasting menu

The historic restaurant of Oviedo — first Michelin star in Asturias, 1974. Same year as Arzak. Seasonal tasting menu that leans into Cantabrian seafood and mountain produce.

Order the casadielles at the end. Non-negotiable.
Night 1 · Oviedo (alternative)
La Corte de Pelayo
Traditional Asturian

The classic Oviedo table. Famous for fabada and cachopo. Finalist in the 2018 Fabada World Championships. Louder, more fun, better for a first night catching up with a friend.

The cachopo recipe from 1947. ~1kg. Share one.
Night 1 · Cider street
Tierra Astur (Gascona)
Sidrería

Calle Gascona is Oviedo's cider street. Tierra Astur is the institution — wooden barrel walls, stone floors, cheese boards, meat. Go here after dinner to drink cider properly.

Try the tortos — Asturian corn tortillas with blue cheese and egg.
Day 2 · Picos (lunch)
Aravalle picnic
Included in the ride

Lunch in a shepherd's cabin is included in the 3-hour Aravalle horse ride. Local cheeses, empanada, cured meats, Picón cheese. Eat it after the mountain climb. It tastes better that way.

Picón cheese from Bejes — different cave to Cabrales, equally good.
Night 3 · Santander
El Serbal
Michelin Star · Seafood

The top table in Santander. Focused on Cantabrian seafood with modern technique. October is a great time — the produce is exceptional and the restaurant is not overrun.

Book at least 2 weeks ahead for October weekends.
Night 3 · Santander (alternative)
Puerto Chico marisquería
Seafood · No reservations

Walk the Puerto Chico area and pick whichever place has the best percebes in the window. Less formal, equally good. Order by the kilo, drink Albariño.

Best for: when you've been on horses all day and just want cold beer and good fish.
